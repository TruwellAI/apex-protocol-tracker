03 / MECHANISM

How Melanotan I Works

01
Binds MC1-R on Melanocytes
MT-I is a synthetic copy of α-MSH — a hormone your body already makes. It selectively binds to MC1-R receptors on melanocytes (pigment-producing cells in your skin). Every human has roughly the same NUMBER of melanocytes — MT-I makes them MORE ACTIVE.
02
Activates cAMP → MITF → Tyrosinase Pathway
MC1-R activation triggers a signaling cascade: cAMP increases → MITF transcription factor activates → tyrosinase enzyme production ramps up. Tyrosinase is the rate-limiting enzyme for melanin production. Once elevated, melanin production continues for DAYS after the MT-I has cleared your blood.
03
Increases EUMELANIN Specifically
Two types of melanin: eumelanin (brown/black — protective) and pheomelanin (red/yellow — actually INCREASES UV damage). MT-I specifically increases eumelanin production. This is critical — you're not just getting darker, you're getting darker with the RIGHT type of melanin.
04
Melanosomes Shield Your DNA
Melanocytes package eumelanin into melanosomes and transfer them to surrounding skin cells. These melanosomes position themselves ABOVE the cell nucleus like tiny umbrellas — physically shielding your DNA from UV radiation. This is your body's built-in defense. MT-I turns up the volume.

01
Longer Time to Sunburn
Research shows MT-I increases the Minimum Erythemal Dose (MED) by 50-75% in fair-skinned individuals. Someone who burns in 20 minutes may not burn until 35-40 minutes. Combined with sunscreen, you get protection from BOTH outside (SPF) and inside (melanin).
02
UV Absorption + Free Radical Scavenging
Eumelanin absorbs UV photons directly, converting UV energy into harmless heat with >99.9% efficiency. It also neutralizes reactive oxygen species (ROS) generated by UV exposure — the same free radicals that cause skin aging and DNA mutations.
03
Reduced UV-Induced DNA Damage
Higher eumelanin content correlates with 10-100× lower rates of UV-induced DNA mutations. Eumelanin also supports faster repair of cyclobutane pyrimidine dimers (CPDs) — the DNA lesions that lead to skin cancer mutations.
MT-I is NOT sunscreen. It enhances your body's natural UV defense but does not replace sunscreen, protective clothing, or shade. Use MT-I AS PART OF a comprehensive sun protection strategy, not instead of one. Always monitor moles and freckles — get 6-month skin checks while using any melanocortin peptide.

06 / SEASONAL STRATEGY

Year-Round Protocol

🌱 SPRING (March–April)
Start loading phase. Build your base tan before summer UV ramps up. This is the ideal time to begin — melanin takes 2-3 weeks to fully develop.
☀️ SUMMER (May–September)
Maintenance 2×/week. Your melanin is at peak. 30 minutes of sun does what 2-3 hours used to. Combined UV protection from melanin + sunscreen = maximum defense.
🍂 FALL (October)
Taper to 1×/week. UV exposure drops naturally. Your tan will hold longer than without MT-I. Optional to continue through fall.
❄️ WINTER (November–February)
Off season. Stop MT-I. Pigmentation fades naturally over 4-8 weeks. No need to maintain a tan when UV exposure is minimal. Resume in March.
07 / SIDE EFFECTS

What to Expect

⚡
Nausea (mild — loading phase only)
Most common side effect. Usually resolves within 30-60 minutes. Significantly milder than MT-II. Dose-dependent — staying at 250-500 mcg minimizes this.
⚡
Facial Flushing
Temporary warmth/redness in the face. Histamine-related. Usually resolves within 1-2 hours. More common during loading.
⚠️
Darkening of Moles & Freckles
Existing moles and freckles may darken. This is expected — melanocytes in those areas are already more active. Monitor all moles for changes in shape, border, or asymmetry. Get a skin check every 6 months while using any melanocortin peptide.
What MT-I does NOT cause (unlike MT-II):
❌ No spontaneous erections
❌ No sexual arousal changes
❌ No appetite suppression
❌ No yawning/fatigue
❌ Does not cross the blood-brain barrier
