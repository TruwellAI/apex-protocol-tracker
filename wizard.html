Step 2 · 60-second primer

The basics, without the textbook.

Four things every peptide user needs to know. Takes a minute. Then we'll show you the actual peptides.

🧬

What peptides are (& how they arrive)

Peptides are short signaling proteins your body already makes — natural messengers that tell tissues what to do. Research peptides arrive as a freeze-dried powder in a small glass vial. Before you inject, you reconstitute it with sterile liquid: bacteriostatic water for most peptides, or acetic acid for a few (AOD-9604, Melanotan). The app tells you which one and exactly how much to add.

💉

How you take them

Most peptides go under the skin (subcutaneous, "subq") — belly fat, an inch or two from the navel. Tiny insulin-grade needle, takes two seconds, barely feels like anything. A handful are nasal sprays. Apex's injection pen does the math automatically — dial the dose, click, done.

⏰

Shelf life matters

Once you mix the powder with BAC water, your peptide has a clock on it — typically 14 to 30 days, stored in the fridge. Past that window it starts breaking down. The app tracks expiry per peptide so you never inject a dead batch. Unmixed vials last years at room temp.

⚠️

Three things to never do

1) Don't freeze reconstituted peptides — ice crystals destroy them.
2) Don't shake the vial hard — swirl gently.
3) Don't stack peptides that do the same thing (we'll warn you if you try).
